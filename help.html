Bhutan Tourism Product Development Guidelines

Basic principles of product development

Some of the principles that may be considered while developing tourism products are:

  1. The development of tourism products to contribute in realizing the goal of Gross National Happiness (GNH).
  2. Accord importance to key principles of sustainable tourism development
  3. Consider conducting basic market research while developing proposals.
  4. Promote tourism circuits, zones or clusters of attractions

Applicability

This guideline shall apply to individuals, CSOs, public and private organizations and agencies interested in the development of tourism products.


General Procedure

TThe following steps need to be followed if the proponent desires know, before developing a detailed proposal, whether the proposed product is permitted:

  1. Submission of Expression of Interest (EOI)
  2. Review of EOI by TCB
  3. Submission of detailed proposal
  4. Review of proposal
  5. Business Licensing

Checklist: Applying for tour guide license

Description

Requirement:

  1. Class twelve or equivalent (Submit mark sheets & school leaving certificate)
  2. Minimum 18 years of age
  3. Valid Bhutanese citizenship identity card
  4. Valid security clearance certificate
  5. Valid medical certificate
  6. Should not be employed in government agencies

Registration for guide course

Register with the following training institutes:

  1. Bhutan International School for Hospitality and Tourism (BISHT), Taba, Thimphu
  2. Institute of Management Studies (IMS), Olakha, Thimphu
  3. Institute of Professional Studies (IPS), Opposite to Bhutan Post, Thimphu
  4. Bhutan Institute of Information Technologies and Management, Paro Town

Guidelines for the Management of Domestic Tourism 2020

Tourism Council of Bhutan

The Tourism Council of Bhutan shall:

  1. Lead in implementing the provisions of the guidelines in coordination with relevant agencies;
  2. Create awareness on the guidelines through relevant agencies;
  3. Monitor service providers, domestic tourists and attraction sites in coordination with relevant stakeholders;
  4. Review and amend these guidelines as and when required; and
  5. Receive and resolve complaints related to the conduct of domestic tourism activities in consultation with relevant stakeholders.

Requirements

In the case of package tour, the following components, agreed between service providers and domestic tourists, shall be included in part or in whole but not limited to:

  1. Tour activities;
  2. Duration of visit;
  3. Accommodation plan;
  4. Guide services;
  5. Transportation including domestic flights and helicopter services;
  6. Trekking haulage;
  7. Meal plan;
  8. Total price of the package;
  9. Cancellation and refund policy; and
  10. Cost in case of emergency evacuation.

Guide Services

If the tour package includes guide services, service providers shall:

  1. Provide details of the guide to the domestic tourists prior to departure;
  2. Ensure the guide communicates in the language understandable to domestic tourists;
  3. Ensure the guide provides information regarding safety measures to domestic tourists; and
  4. Ensure the guide has knowledge and experience of sites identified in the package.
