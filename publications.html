Feasibility Study to Promote Birding Tourism in Samdrup Jongkhar, Tsirang and Gasa

Project Objective:

Enhance regional spread of tourism by identifying birding potentialities in Samdrup Jongkhar, Sarpang-Tsirang & Punakha-Gasa Dzongkhags (2019 – 2020 FYP)

Abstract

This bird survey activity was executed based on the July 2019 agreement between the Secretariat, Tourism Council of Bhutan and the Ugyen Wangchuck Institute for Conservation and Environment Research to promote and expand nature-based tourism in Bhutan and the region. The surveys were conducted along three potential bird watching routes namely: Narphu – Samdrup Jongkhar, Shechamthang – Waklaytar and Rimchu – Gasa Dzong in three consecutive seasons (Fall, Winter and Spring, 2019 – 2020). With our work and literature reviews, we recognize, the Narphu – Samdrup Jongkhar (368 bird species) bird watching route as the best for birding activity followed by Shechamthang – Waklaytar (312 bird species) and Rimchu to Gasa (205 bird species).

Introduction

The Himalayan Kingdom of Bhutan is blessed with its geophysical factors, visionary monarchs and people to be endowed with rich biodiversity. Owing to this natural gift of biodiversity, Bhutan is identified as a part of the ten global hotspots on earth. Today, Bhutan has recorded a total of 748 bird species. Given the size disparity, indeed, it is stunning to have bird species richness comparable to North America.

Tourism in Bhutan

Tourism in Bhutan is guided by the sound policy of ‘High Value, Low Volume’. Bhutan took this unique approach since the opening of tourism in the country in 1974. In line with this policy, tourism is managed in a sustainable manner without leading to negative impacts on the environment and culture among others. Today, Bhutan is recognized as one of the sustainable, high end tourist destinations in the world.

Village Home Stays (VHS)

In Bhutan, visitors have the option to stay in village home stays approved by the Tourism Council of Bhutan. As the name suggests, village homestays are promoted mainly in rural areas to offer basic accommodation facilities to visitors. It is an opportunity to generate supplementary income and improve the livelihoods in the rural communities where farming is the main source of income.

REPORT ON BHUTAN'S PARTICIPATION (TOURISM) IN WORLD EXPO 2020, DUBAI

The World Expo 2020 held in Dubai is the first event of such kind to be held in MEASA region. It was originally scheduled to take place from 1st October 2020 to 31st March 2021. However as the world was struck by COVID-19 pandemic and various calamities following it, the expo was also delayed by one year and it was rescheduled from 1st October 2021 to 31st March 2022.
